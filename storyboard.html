STORYBOARD

Digital Kiosk

Background

Kazim, a refugee who has recently arrived in Ireland, has learned about an innovative new service offered at his local public library from a fellow refugee who has lived here for a few years. This service provides an opportunity for individuals like Kazim to actively participate in the public housing decision-making process. Through a user-friendly digital kiosk installed in the library, Kazim can access and engage with information related to public housing initiatives, projects, and community developments. The digital kiosk serves as a bridge that connects residents, including newcomers like Kazim, with local government initiatives and decision-making processes.

1.

Before going to the library, Kazim decides to do some research into the benefits of this new system online.

2.

Once Kazim knows a little more he visits the library to investigate further.

3.

In the library he finds printed materials detailing the advantages of the new system and how to use the new system.

4.

Kazim talks to a staff member and asks him questions about the new system.

5.

He even attends a community event hosted at the library announcing the launch of the new service.

6.

Happy that he can benefit from using this new service, Kazim returns to the library.

7.

Upon arrival, he must locate the new kiosk and notices it at the front of the library under bright, new signage.

8.

Kazim sits down and interacts with the touch screen kiosk for the very first time.

9.

The first step is to choose his preferred language. This will help alleviate any language barriers.

10.

After choosing his preferred language and completing the short onboarding process, Kazim lands on the main menu.

11.

He views the info section where he can keep up-to-date with housing schemes, see a list of upcoming events, useful links, legal regulations and applications forms.

12.

He visits the contact page where he contact the government department responsible for public housing schemes directly of locate the closest office.

13.

But Kazim is here to leave feedback on his experiences hoping to shape future processes. He creates an account, navigates to the feedback section, and leaves his comments.

14.

Once he submits his feedback he is asked whether he would like to receive updates on the project he has fed back on. Updates are available directly from the kiosk, email and text message.

15.

Once he chooses his contact preferences and confirms the kiosk displays other library initiatives that might be relevant to the user.

16.

Kazim is really pleased with how easy-to-use and intuitive the system is.

17.

Kazim receives an update on his phone about his feedback. It has been received by the local agency and is being considered. Kazim feels proud

18.

He returns to the library to view how his feedback has been implemented.

19.

He logs into the kiosk using the account he previously set up. His language preferences have been saved.

20.

When he logs in he can see a notification appearing giving him updates on the project he supplied feedback on and letting him know how his feedback was used.
